# Reflection:

### Initial Approach and the GIGO Principle
When I first started this assignment, I went with what I’d call "vibe coding." I wrote basic system prompts like *"You are Abhimanyu, give career advice"* or *"You are Anshuman, be a direct tech lead."* Unsurprisingly, the results were mediocre. The AI gave boilerplate responses that felt like a generic career coach. This was a classic case of **GIGO (Garbage In, Garbage Out)** because my input lacked specific depth and research, the LLM’s output was shallow and lacked personality.

### The Research Process
To fix the "garbage input" issue, I stepped away from the code and spent time on research. I watched several hours of content in youtube get more data, specifically the *Almost Engineers* podcast and the *Raj Shamani* interview. Hearing the founders talk about their own journeys, their "Compass vs. Map" philosophy, and their specific gripes with the current education system gave me the data points I needed. 

I realized that prompt engineering isn't just about the instructions; it’s about providing the right **contextual anchors**. For example, adding Abhimanyu’s focus on "Learning Delta" or Kshitij sir’s "Activation Energy" analogy immediately shifted the AI from a chatbot to a persona.

### Replicating My Instructors
Writing the prompt for Kshitij sir was a personal challenge for me. Since he is one of my actual instructors at Scaler, I have a firsthand understanding of his teaching style. He has this very specific vibe he’s incredibly calm and patient, but also very observant and structured in how he breaks down LLD and DSA. I tried really hard to replicate that "Zen Master" instructor feel in his system prompt, focusing on his emphasis on consistency and mastering one thing at a time rather than just providing a solution. I also tried to incorporating his sarcastic-friendly jokes and laugh on logical issues in approach/code.

### Conclusion
This assignment taught me that building a "working" chatbot is easy, but building an "authentic" one requires a deep understanding of the human behind the prompt. Moving from generic instructions to researched, high-fidelity personas made all the difference in the final product.
